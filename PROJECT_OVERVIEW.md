# 10,000 Years of Solitude - Project Overview

## Core Concept
A global "Groundhog Day" scenario where the entire world is trapped reliving the same calendar date (October 23rd) indefinitely. Each reset is called an "iteration" and is numbered sequentially. Different characters have experienced different numbers of iterations, creating a complex narrative structure.

## Narrative Structure
- **Multiple POV chapters**: Each chapter follows a different character
- **Iteration numbering**: Each chapter begins with the iteration number that character is experiencing
- **Same calendar date**: All events occur on October 23rd (with one exception noted in Jennifer's Egypt story)
- **Varying experience levels**: Characters range from early iterations (Sydney: ~5 days) to extreme veterans (Caius: 350+ years worth)
- **The convergence**: The protagonists spend most of the book moving through the same loop without seeing each other. Caius's experiment (Project Dandelion) is the structural gravity well — his slow recruitment pulls every POV character in, and through it they discover each other's loops. See THE_BIBLE.md §8, "The Protagonist Geometry."
- **The protagonist axis**: Meredith wants ESCAPE; Caius wants COMPREHENSION. Fundamentally different stories pulling in opposite directions through the same loop — and her nihilism is, unknown to her forever, the seed of his quest.

## Key Characters
- **Meredith**: Told her therapist on October 22nd she would kill herself that night; the loop traps her in the day after. The escape pole of the novel. Her despair secretly catalyzes everything
- **Caius**: Ancient veteran (~350 years of iterations), orchestrates complex plans; wants a working model of the universe with no stray variables. The comprehension pole
- **Jonas**: Victim of Caius's elaborate psychological torture scheme
- **Jennifer**: Cruise ship passenger, experiences nihilistic breakdown
- **Jeff/Thud**: Tree-through-skull death loop; becomes the searcher who discovers going to glass
- **Satoshi Furukawa**: Physician-astronaut aboard the ISS at checkpoint; orbital experimentalist at the degradation boundary
- **Sydney**: Airline passenger, early in her iteration experience
- **The Baby**: Unnamed infant who develops adult cognition while trapped in baby body

## Central Mystery
The cause of the time loop - explored through CERN scientists investigating whether reality is a simulation with a critical bug.

## Themes
- Existential horror of infinite repetition
- Human adaptability and breakdown under extreme circumstances  
- The nature of consciousness, memory, and identity
- Scientific vs. supernatural explanations for impossible phenomena
- Agency vs. predetermination: the experiment "works" and makes everything categorically worse — the agents trade infinite loops with agency for one frozen moment of infinite duration
- A horror novel that reads like life until the last chapter (see THE_BIBLE.md §10: tone contract, pressure valves, the Rorschach ending)

## Current Status
Early manuscript phase with significant logical inconsistencies that need resolution before proceeding with character development and tone refinement.
