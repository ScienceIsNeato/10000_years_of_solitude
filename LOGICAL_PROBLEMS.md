## CRITICAL INCONSISTENCIES

### 1. Start Time Synchronization Issue
**Problem**: Unclear whether all humans reset simultaneously or at distributed times

**Status**: ✅ RESOLVED
**Resolution**: All agents reset simultaneously at 00:00:00 UTC. No staggering. What varies is local time (7 PM New York, 5 PM Pacific, 8 AM Tokyo). See THE_BIBLE.md §3, "Reset Timing."

### 2. Memory Formation vs. Brain Reset Paradox  
**Problem**: Memories persist across iterations while brains physically reset

**Status**: ✅ RESOLVED
**Resolution**: Dual-engine architecture. The physics engine (including brains) resets completely. The agent engine (consciousness, memory, subjective experience) does NOT reset — it runs on separate computational substrate. Memory persists because it's not stored in the physics engine's neural architecture; it's stored in the agent engine. No neurogenesis, no hippocampal growth, no quantum entanglement hand-waving needed. See THE_BIBLE.md §1 and §3.

**Note**: The exact neuroscience of the mismatch between engines is deliberately left partially unresolved within the novel. Characters can theorize about it. Accumulating anomalies (novel mental phenomena increasing with iteration count) are a feature, not a bug — they create narrative pressure.

### 4. The Baby Development Impossibility
**Problem**: Infant develops adult cognition while remaining physically infantile

**Status**: ✅ RESOLVED (and improved)
**Resolution**: Agent engine develops independently of the physics engine. The baby's consciousness grows, but grows from *newborn-grade sensory input only*. This is an ultra-Helen Keller situation: severely limited sensory vocabulary (blurred vision, fragmented sound, no mobility, no language capacity, plus underdeveloped internal senses). The mind that emerges may not resemble adult human consciousness at all — it may be something genuinely alien, building cognitive structures from a sensory world no other human shares. See THE_BIBLE.md §8 (Character Registry, Bura Samay).

### 6. Variable Initial Conditions
**Problem**: Some "initial conditions" can change, others cannot

**Status**: ✅ RESOLVED
**Resolution**: Physics engine is deterministic from the same RNG seed. Everything before conscious perturbation is identical every iteration. After agents wake and make different choices (because they remember previous iterations), their perturbations inject different inputs into the physics engine, causing butterfly effects. Jeff CAN be saved from the tree — but only if an agent (Caius) arrives in time to change the physical conditions. The tree falls identically every iteration; whether Jeff is in the car when it lands depends on what agents do. See THE_BIBLE.md §3, "The Coupling After Reset."

### 13. Multi-Agent Synchronization and the Rollback Moment
**Status**: ❓ OPEN — needs author confirmation
**Problem**: THE_BIBLE.md §3 makes the reset per-agent (loss of consciousness = your return to checkpoint), with individual loop durations and an arbitrary, non-midnight checkpoint time. It does not specify when the shared physics engine rolls back, given that agents lose consciousness at different times (Jennifer at hour 16, Jeff at week 3, a glass-seeker at month 2 — all sharing one world).
**Working model adopted in the wiki articles** (derivable from the bible, but confirm): the physics run continues as long as any agent remains conscious; agents whose loops close experience a jump cut and re-enter at the next checkpoint, all together; the rollback itself is unobservable in principle, because anyone in a position to observe it hasn't rolled back yet. Consequences embraced by the articles: society shares only the first ~16–20 hours after checkpoint (the "shared hours"); long-wakers inhabit a progressively depopulated world (this is now the stated constraint on geodesic travel — the world's aviation falls asleep, replacing the old "be on the ground at midnight" rule, which was an artifact of the stale midnight model); iteration length is technically variable and set by the most stubborn consciousness.
**Corollary — abandoned bodies**: once an agent's consciousness lapses mid-loop, their body cannot be woken by any stimulus (the agent engine has departed; "no nap has ever been woken from"), though autonomic function continues normally. This is asymmetric with checkpoint sleepers (#15), who CAN be woken because their agent engine is attached and waiting for the body to surface. The Furukawa chapter ("Sixteen Sunrises") dramatizes both sides: he wakes naturally minutes after checkpoint each loop, and his sleeping crewmates are unwakeable "warm sites of absence" during his runs. Confirm the asymmetry.
**Note**: This supersedes the previous #13 ("midnight transit mechanics"), which was framed against the stale synchronized-midnight model and is void under THE_BIBLE.md §3.
